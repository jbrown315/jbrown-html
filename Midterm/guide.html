CPU and RAM Install: After unpacking the mother board, locate the CPU socket and undo the clamp. Carefully put the the CPU into the socket, lining up the corners with the proper labels and clamp it down. Then move over to the RAM slots and unlatch the appropriate slot. Push in RAM until it clicks. Assuming everything seems snug and in place then this step was completed successfully.

CPU Cooler Install: Unpack the CPU cooler and set it aside. After completing the CPU and RAM install get the motherboard and install the mounting bracket for the cooler around the CPU socket. Apply a small dot of thermal paste to the top of the CPU. Once the bracket is installed screw down the cooler onto the CPU. Then plug the fan cable into the motherboard port labeled "cpu". The cooler is now installed.

GPU Install: Unpack the graphics card and put the computer on its side. Unscrew the side ports so the card will fit in the computer. Insert the graphics card into the PCI-E slot in the motherboard. Once the graphics card is lined up with the side of the case, screw it into the case. Some cards will require extra power so make sure to connect that if needed.

Power Supply Install: Unpack the power supply and any cables that come with it. Align the power supply with the specified spot in the case. Screw in the power supply making sure that it is completely fastened into the case. After it is screwed in, attach all power cables to the specified parts. Follow the manuals that came with your parts for specific instructions on where to connect the cables.

Hard Drive Install: Unpack the hard drive and make sure to handle it carefully because it is very fragile. Place it into the specified drive bay and fasten it in with the provided screws. One attached, connect the drive to power and then attach the SATA cable. Then connect the other end of the SATA cable into the motherboard to complete the hard drive installation.
