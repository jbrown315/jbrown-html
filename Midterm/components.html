This is a CPU, also known as the central processing unit or processor for short. Many will argue that the processor is the most important component in a computer. The processor is the brain of the computer. It performs arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output operations specified by the user.

This is a main board, also known as the motherboard. The motherboard is the central hub of the computer. It brings together all other parts of the computer and connects them as well giving them power. Another task of the motherboard includes storing the key of your operating system. The motherboard is an essential piece of any computer because it facilitates the communication between other components.

This is Random Access Memory, also known as RAM. RAM is a form of memory for a computer, which is not to be confused with storage. RAM is like a human's short term memory, it stores the information that the computer is currently using. RAM can be easily swapped in and out of a computer. Most computers these days have 8gb or 16gb of RAM. Advanced computers for gaming or servers can have upwards of 32gb of RAM.

This is a Graphics Processing Unit, also known as a graphics card. The graphics card is responsible for the video out put from a computer. Even though many processors come with integrated graphics now, a graphics card causes a dramatic increase in video quality. Graphics cards can range in price from around $300 all the way to $1500.

This is a power supply unit, also known as a power supply. The power supply is crucial to the computer because with out it the computer would have no electricity. The amount of power you need is dependent on the other components that you have in your computer. Most power supply's these days range in wattage from about 100w to over 1200w.

This is a hard disk drive, also known as the hard drive. The hard drive is where you store all of you data on your computer. Hard drives can store all types of data including audio files, videos, games and many other file forms. Common hard drives can have any amount of storage capacity, however lately, the largest hard dives have about 12TB of storage.
